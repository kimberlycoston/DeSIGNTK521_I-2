- Missing directions: No distribution guidance led to overuse of a few mechanisms and under-exploration of moments in the decision journey (before/during/after/ongoing). No tags to cluster by stage or fear type.
- Weak Ring mapping: Although each idea names a Ring mechanism, the mapping is often implied rather than explicit (‚Äúwhat Motion Alerts mean in investing‚Äù is not consistently spelled out).
- Safety mechanism clarity: Some one-liners clearly reduce anxiety (10, 13), others are generic reassurance without stating the specific uncertainty reduced (6, 21).
- Emotional safety gaps: Social features (5, 22) could increase shame if not tightly scoped; the prompt didn‚Äôt require naming which fear is addressed, so ‚Äúsafety‚Äù is broad and diffuse.
- Feasibility: Most are plausible, but some depend on open-ended NLP or advisory gray areas (6, 14) without constraints to keep them implementable and compliant.
- Clustering difficulty: Without tags for moment or fear type, the set is harder to organize and compare.

Prompt causes:
- It asks to use Ring-like mechanisms but doesn‚Äôt enforce explicit mechanism-to-analog mapping.
- No distribution constraints across Ring features or decision moments.
- No requirement to declare which fear is targeted, so ‚Äúsafety‚Äù stays abstract.
- No instruction to specify concrete triggers/inputs and outputs, enabling vague interactions.
- No feasibility or compliance guardrails, allowing ideas that drift into advisory or complex AI.
- Output schema leaves little structure for clustering or deduplication.
